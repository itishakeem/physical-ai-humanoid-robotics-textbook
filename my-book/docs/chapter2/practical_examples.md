# Chapter 2: Humanoid Robotics Fundamentals - Practical Examples

This chapter highlights several iconic humanoid robots that have pushed the boundaries of what these machines can achieve, showcasing the practical application of the fundamental principles discussed.

## ASIMO (Advanced Step in Innovative Mobility) by Honda

ASIMO is one of the most recognized humanoid robots globally, developed by Honda. Its development began in 1986, with the first ASIMO model introduced in 2000.
-   **Capabilities:** ASIMO demonstrated remarkable advancements in bipedal locomotion, including walking, running, climbing stairs, and even dancing. It could recognize faces, understand human speech, and interact with its environment through hand gestures and object manipulation.
-   **Impact:** ASIMO's development significantly contributed to research in dynamic balance, gait generation, and human-robot interaction. It showcased the potential for humanoids in assistive roles, education, and public relations, paving the way for future generations of humanoid robots.

## Atlas by Boston Dynamics

Atlas is a highly advanced humanoid robot renowned for its dynamic and agile movements, developed by Boston Dynamics.
-   **Capabilities:** Atlas is designed for robust outdoor mobile manipulation. It can perform complex acrobatic feats like running, jumping, parkour, and even backflips, demonstrating unparalleled dynamic balance and whole-body control. It uses hydraulic actuation to achieve high power and speed.
-   **Impact:** Atlas serves as a research platform to explore the limits of mobile dexterity. Its videos have often gone viral, pushing public perception of what robots are capable of and inspiring new research into highly dynamic and agile robotics in unstructured environments.

## Sophia by Hanson Robotics

Sophia is a social humanoid robot developed by Hanson Robotics, known for its remarkably human-like appearance and ability to engage in conversation.
-   **Capabilities:** Sophia combines advanced facial expressions (using patented Frubber skin and mechanisms) with AI for natural language processing, allowing for human-like conversations and emotional responses. It can track faces, maintain eye contact, and process speech.
-   **Impact:** Sophia highlights the advancements in human-robot interaction, particularly in creating emotionally resonant and socially engaging robots. While its cognitive abilities are often debated, it serves as an important platform for exploring the social dimensions of AI and robotics, and the potential for humanoids in customer service, education, and companionship.

## Digit by Agility Robotics

Digit is a bipedal robot designed for logistics and last-mile delivery, developed by Agility Robotics.
-   **Capabilities:** Digit is built for efficient bipedal locomotion and manipulation. It can walk, balance on one leg, and carry packages. Its design emphasizes robustness and the ability to navigate human environments like warehouses and sidewalks.
-   **Impact:** Digit represents a commercial-focused approach to humanoid robotics, aiming to solve real-world problems in logistics and package handling. It showcases how humanoids can integrate into existing human infrastructure for practical applications.

These examples demonstrate the diverse applications and continuously evolving capabilities of humanoid robots, from groundbreaking research platforms to socially interactive companions and practical commercial assistants.
